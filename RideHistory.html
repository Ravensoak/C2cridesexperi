2025 â€“ Scottish Borders

Overview

A route from Gretna Green to Berwick-on-Tweed, following parts of the 'Borderers Way'.

  • Day 1 - Gretna to Carlisle: The ride started in the afternoon with a short route from Gretna Green into Carlisle.
  • Day 2 - Carlisle to Hawick: The route followed quiet country lanes and scenic roads crossing the England / Scotland border before reaching Hawick.
  • Day 3 - Hawick to Wooler: Passing through Kelso allowing us to witness the Women's Tour of Britain before skirting the Cheviot Hills and arriving in Wooler.

2024 â€“ Scotland

Overview

A circular route starting and ending near Edinburgh, passing through Stirling and Glasgow.

  • Day 1 - Champany to Stirling: The ride started by visiting the 3 bridge viewpoint in Queensferry before crossing the Forth road bridge and riding the shoreline of the Firth Estuary making use of cycle paths through Dunfermline to Stirling.
  • Day 2 - Stirling to Glasgow: The route headed to the shores of Loch Lomond (with the option to cross Loch Lomond via a ferry) before using cycle paths and approaching Glasgow following the River Clyde.
  • Day 3 - Glasgow to Champany: The route back to Champany followed cycle paths allowing a visit to the Falkirk Wheel, the Kelpies and Blackness Castle along the way.

2018 â€“ South Wales to North Wales

Overview

A challenging route starting on the South Wales coast in Llanelli, passing the side of the Brecon Beacons and the Elan Valley, through the heart of Wales and Snowdonia to finish in Conwy on the North Wales coast.

  • Day 1 - Llanelli to Llandovery: The ride started in the afternoon from Llanelli on a disused railway line, passing Carreg Cennon Castle before reaching Llandovery.
  • Day 2 - Llandovery to Staylittle: The route took us through Builth Wells and Rhayader before a challenging few climbs to reach our accommodation at the 'Weather Station' hostel in Staylittle.
  • Day 3 - Staylittle to Porthmadog: Heading North West from Staylittle, this route had a steep climb after Machynlleth passing Corris before descending to Dolgellau and along the Mawddach Trail, crossing the Barmouth Railway bridge and through Harlech onto Porthmadog.
  • Day 4 - Porthmadog to Conwy: The route took us through Ffestiniog and up to the Ffynnon Eidda viewpoint before descending towards PenMachno and Betws-y-Coed and then following the Conwy valley down to the coast at Conwy.

2017 â€“ Coast to Coast

Overview

One of the classic Sustrans Coast to Coast routes from the Irish Sea to the North Sea, crossing the Lake District, Pennines, and Durham Dales.

  • Day 1 - Whitehaven to Penrith: The route starts on the West coast and took us through the Northern part of the lake district, over Whinlatter Pass and into Keswick. It should take the scenic Threlkeld railway path but flood damage forces a detour past Castlerigg Stone Circle with the first day ending in Penrith.
  • Day 2 - Penrith to Allenheads: A few climbs including Hartside Pass and we were into wild morland of the West Penines. Passing by Garrigill Falls and onto Nenthead before climbing to our destination Allenheads in the heart of the Pennines.
  • Day 3 - Allenheads to Sunderland: A steep climb out of Allenheads leads across the county border to a beautiful descent to Stanhope before another steep climb to Waskerley. From here it is mostly downhill and cyclepaths into Sunderland finishing on Roker Beach.

2016 â€“ Reivers Way

Overview

This is a Sustrans route from Whitehaven to Tynemouth tracing the historic paths of the Border Reivers - raiders who operated along the Angloâ€”Scottish border from the 13th to 17th centuries.

  • Day 1 - Whitehaven to Carlisle: The route makes its way inland to the North West part of the Lake District with Carlisle being our Day 1 destination.
  • Day 2 - Carlisle to Bellingham: Soon after Carlisle we make our way to the Scottish Border where we ride through wild rugged countryside passing Kielder Water before reaching our final destiantion in Bellingham.
  • Day 3 - Bellingham to Tynemouth: The route passes through beautiful countryside before arriving at the North Sea coast above Whitley Bay. From here, it is a short route down the coastal road to Tynemouth.

2015 â€“ Hadrians Wall Cycleway

Overview

This is a Sustrans route from Ravenglass to South Shields following the line of Hadrianâ€™s Wall from the Irish Sea to the North Sea, offering a blend of Roman history, scenic countryside, and quiet roads.

  • Day 1 - Ravenglass to Silloth: The route starts at the roman fort in Ravenglass and follows the coastal path behind Sellafield Nuclear Plant and then skirting the North West part of the Lake District passing through Cockermouth and onto our Day 1 end destination of Silloth.
